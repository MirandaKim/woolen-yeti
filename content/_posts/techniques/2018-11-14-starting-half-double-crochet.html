About this stitch

{% include image.html src="/assets/images/techniques/shdc_finished.png" srcLow="/assets/images/techniques/shdc_finished-10px.png" alt="Half double crochet swatch using starting half double crochets along the edges." classStr="image--fit-to-width image--shadow-frame"%}

Here is a nice stitch to replace the chain 2 when starting a new row of half double crochets (U.S.). Itâ€™s about the same size as a standard half double and leaves you with a couple of easily accessible loops at the top for you to work your next row into.

It's pretty quickly once you get the hang of it.

How to do it:

  1. Single crochet.
  2. Insert hook under the front, inner-most, vertical loop created by the single crochet in step 1 (see arrow in step 1 of images below). Yarn over and pull through twice to complete a single crochet.
  3. You should now have a stitch with similar width and height to a half double crochet.
